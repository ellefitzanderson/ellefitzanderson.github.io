About

The person behind
the practice.

I build product organisations and the people inside them. The skills matter, but the real work is the surrounding system: the decision rights, the funding model, and the habits that decide whether new capability survives contact with the org chart.

Twenty-five years inside product development environments, across LEGO, Bupa, Kraft Heinz, Capital One, Lloyds, Tesco Bank, Bloomberg, Domino's, and Walmart, means that when a product manager tells me what is really getting in their way, it is rarely news. My work since 2018 has centred on data science and machine learning products, and lately on how product people actually adopt AI: where it sticks, where it stalls, and what moves a team from AI-curious to AI-fluent.

I am an American who has made the UK my home for over 20 years. And I test everything on myself first: the AI coach my training cohorts use began as the one I built for my own work.

The other half of my practice is people. I lead strong product functions and stretch capable product people into leaders who solve complex problems and delight customers. My method is manager as coach, built on ICF and ICAgile qualifications and a simple conviction:

The people in front of me are beyond capable. They don't need fixing, they need stretching, so their ideas can reach the next level.

And I think with my hands. I'm a trained visual facilitator and an illustrator, the video on the front page is me at my craft table, because drawing the problem is often how a room finally sees it. It's an unusual string to a product leader's bow, and it changes what workshops, strategy sessions and leadership offsites can do.
