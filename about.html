About

The person behind
the practice.

I'm an American who made the UK home. Long enough ago that I've stopped apologising (apologizing?) for reaching for the wrong spelling — my working life has run through London boardrooms and American product organisations, and the mid-Atlantic habits are permanent. What that mix actually gives you: someone equally at ease with US pace and UK nuance, who has led product for household names on both sides of the water.

I lead product functions and transformations for large organisations — banking, retail, healthcare, consumer goods — usually at the moments that matter: post-acquisition, mid-restructure, or when a digital strategy has stalled and needs turning into a shipped roadmap. I carry a working toolkit of product tactics with me, tailored and evolved at every organisation, and I remain hands-on technical: AI-native in daily practice, building in public, with the receipts to review on GitHub.

The other half of my practice is people. I lead strong product functions and stretch capable product people into leaders who solve complex problems and delight customers. My method is manager as coach — built on ICF and ICAgile qualifications and a simple conviction:

The people in front of me are beyond capable. They don't need fixing — they need stretching, so their ideas can reach the next level.

And I think with my hands. I'm a trained visual facilitator and an illustrator — the video on the front page is me at my craft table, because drawing the problem is often how a room finally sees it. It's an unusual string to a product leader's bow, and it changes what workshops, strategy sessions and leadership offsites can do.
